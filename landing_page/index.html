Challenges and what we plan!

‚öñÔ∏è

Utility interconnection takes too long

Medium
Why it matters: Schedules slip and hosts lose interest if timelines drift.
What we do:
  • Phase 1: Go behind-the-meter PPAs where possible to minimize interconnect scope.
  • Phase 2: Pre-apply with utilities, standardize one-line & relay packs to cut review cycles.
  • Phase 3: Batch sites under an aggregation plan to reuse approvals.
üèóÔ∏è

Canopy design, wind load, drainage

Low
Why it matters: Texas wind, storms, and ADA paths need careful detailing.
What we do:
  • Use stamped, repeatable canopy SKUs; integrate gutters to existing storm drains.
  • Lay out columns to keep ADA routes clear; night lighting + CCTV on the structure.
  • Geotech + pull tests early to lock footings and avoid rework.
üí∏

Upfront capex and host sticker shock

Medium
Why it matters: Hosts want savings without spending capital.
What we do:
  • PPA: No upfront cost; host buys power at 8‚Äì10¬¢/kWh, below retail.
  • Tax credits: Use ITC/transferability; optionally bring a tax equity partner.
  • Portfolio finance: Group sites, then refinance or sell to infra funds post-COD.
üß∞

O&M, liability, and uptime

Low
Why it matters: Downtime kills returns; hosts worry about roof-style headaches.
What we do:
  • Remote monitoring + truck rolls under SLAs; vegetation and panel cleaning schedule.
  • Wrap insurance for canopy, signage, and EV assets; clear incident response SOPs.
  • Modular parts and spares; vendor warranties tracked in the portal.

Wholesale price swings and peak timing

Medium
Why it matters: Grid-only sales can be thin outside peaks.
What we do:
  • Phase 1: Lock value with host PPAs (stable cash flow).
  • Phase 2: Add batteries for buy-low / sell-high arbitrage; enroll ancillary services.
  • Phase 3: Aggregate sites into a VPP to smooth volatility.

Credits on bills for renters and SMEs

Medium
Why it matters: Some Texas utilities lack clean "virtual credit" flows.
What we do:
  • Start with hosts first; keep "consumer credits" as a future layer or utility pilot.
  • Where allowed, use on-bill credit partners; else offer direct-pay rebates.
  • Run city or campus programs where billing rails are simpler.

Know more about us

What is parking lot solar?

Parking Lot Solar is a service that enables residential individuals and groups to reserve solar capacity from commercial scale pay-for-use solar projects installed over parking lots, to trade power for bill credits, and in doing so, allows individuals to use these credits to save up on their power bills.

Where are these parking lot solar canopies installed?

Parking Lot Solar Projects can be installed in various locations in Texas where net metering and RESCO projects are permissible. Current systems are installed in Austin and Houston.

Do I need any approval from my utility to use your service?

Utilities that are listed here are enabled for addition of credits to power bills. For them, no additional permissions or requirements are necessary nor needs any changes to the building power meter.

How do I get parking lot solar?

You can reserve Parking Lot Solar capacity from one of our available projects. Once you reserve, the power generated from your reservation will be traded into bill credits which can be used to save up on your monthly power bill.

Can I make reservations in batches?

Yes. You can reserve from the same project or from multiple other projects (if available) in batches. This works great especially for reserving more if power bills increase or want to get accustomed to parking lot solar first with a smaller batch.

How do I connect credits to my power bills?

Simply pay your power bill through AsphaltToEnergy (the process is much like using payment apps such as PayPal and Venmo to pay for power) and credits get automatically added to that due bill.

Can I withdraw my credits as cash?

On-bill credits are used to add on top of power bills to discount them and in present form can't be withdrawn or transferred to a bank account.

How do I know if my parking lot qualifies as a host?

We require a minimum of 2 acres of parking space with good sun exposure. Our team will conduct a free site assessment to determine eligibility.

Do hosts use the energy generated or is it sold to the grid?

Energy is sold back to the grid, not used on-site. This maximizes revenue and provides grid services while hosts benefit from shade and cooling.

What happens if I move or sell my property?

For hosts: The agreement transfers to the new owner. For users: Your credits move with you to any new address we serve.

Are there any hidden fees?

No hidden fees. What you see is what you pay. We believe in transparent, straightforward pricing.
