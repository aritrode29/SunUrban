RISK MITIGATION

Challenges & How We Tackle Them

We've identified the risks and have clear strategies for each. Click to expand.

Utility interconnection takes too long

MEDIUM
Why it matters: Schedules slip and hosts lose interest if timelines drift.
What we do:
  • Phase 1: Go behind-the-meter PPAs to minimize interconnect scope
  • Phase 2: Pre-apply with utilities, standardize relay packs to cut review cycles
  • Phase 3: Batch sites under aggregation plan to reuse approvals

Canopy design, wind load, drainage

LOW
Why it matters: Texas wind, storms, and ADA paths need careful detailing.
What we do:
  • Use stamped, repeatable canopy SKUs; integrate gutters to existing storm drains
  • Lay out columns to keep ADA routes clear; night lighting + CCTV on structure
  • Geotech + pull tests early to lock footings and avoid rework

Upfront capex and host sticker shock

MEDIUM
Why it matters: Hosts want savings without spending capital upfront.
What we do:
  • PPA: No upfront cost; host buys power at 8–10¢/kWh, below retail
  • Tax credits: Use ITC/transferability; optionally bring tax equity partner
  • Portfolio finance: Group sites, then refinance or sell to infra funds post-COD

O&M, liability, and uptime guarantees

LOW
Why it matters: Downtime kills returns; hosts worry about roof-style headaches.
What we do:
  • Remote monitoring + truck rolls under SLAs; vegetation and panel cleaning schedule
  • Wrap insurance for canopy, signage, and EV assets; clear incident response SOPs
  • Modular parts and spares; vendor warranties tracked in portal

Credits on bills for renters and SMEs

MEDIUM
Why it matters: Some Texas utilities lack clean "virtual credit" flows.
What we do:
  • Start with hosts first; keep "consumer credits" as a future layer
  • Where allowed, use on-bill credit partners; else offer direct-pay rebates
  • Run city or campus programs where billing rails are simpler

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SunUrban

What is parking lot solar and how does it work?

SunUrban installs elevated solar canopies over existing parking lots. Here's how it works:

  • Installation: We build steel canopy structures with solar panels on top, providing shade below
  • Power Generation: The panels generate clean electricity during daylight hours
  • Power Purchase: You buy this power at a fixed rate (typically 8-10¢/kWh) — lower than utility rates
  • Savings: Most customers save 15-35% on electricity bills from day one

Bonus: Your parking lot becomes 10-20°C cooler, protecting vehicles and improving customer experience.

Do I need to pay anything upfront?

No upfront cost! Our Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) model means:

  • $0 installation cost — We finance and own the system
  • $0 maintenance cost — We handle all repairs and upkeep
  • $0 insurance cost — We carry full coverage
  • You only pay for the electricity you use, at a rate lower than your utility

Think of it like getting a free parking lot upgrade that also cuts your electricity bills. It's that simple.

How much parking lot space do I need?

Minimum: 2 acres for optimal economics. But we evaluate every site individually.

Here's what we look for:

  • Size: 2+ acres preferred (roughly 100+ parking spaces)
  • Sun exposure: Minimal shading from buildings or trees
  • Ownership: Property owner or long-term lease holder
  • Location: Currently focused on Texas markets

Not sure if you qualify? Use our calculator or contact us for a free site assessment.

What happens during storms or extreme weather?

Our structures are engineered specifically for Texas weather:

  • Wind rated: Designed to withstand 90+ mph winds (Texas building code compliant)
  • Hail resistant: Panels rated for 1-inch hail at 50+ mph
  • Drainage integrated: Gutters route water to existing storm drains
  • Fully insured: We carry comprehensive insurance on all structures

If damage ever occurs, we handle all repairs at no cost to you. That's included in our PPA agreement.

Can you add EV charging stations?

Yes! EV charging is a perfect complement to solar canopies:

  • Level 2 chargers: Ideal for 2-4 hour parking (retail, offices)
  • DC Fast chargers: 15-30 minute charging for high-turnover locations
  • Solar-powered: Chargers run on clean energy from the panels above
  • Revenue opportunity: Charge customers for EV use, creating additional income

Best part? We can include EV chargers in your PPA at no extra upfront cost. You earn revenue from charging fees while we handle the infrastructure.

How long does installation take?

Typical timeline: 8-12 weeks from contract signing to power generation.

  • Week 1-2: Site survey, engineering, and permit applications
  • Week 3-4: Permit approval (we handle all paperwork)
  • Week 5-8: Construction and installation
  • Week 9-10: Utility interconnection and inspection
  • Week 11-12: System commissioning and go-live

Your parking lot remains fully operational during most of the installation — we work section by section to minimize disruption.

What if I sell my property?

The PPA agreement transfers to the new owner — and that's usually a selling point!

  • Built-in value: Solar canopy adds property value and tenant appeal
  • Easy transfer: Simple assignment of the PPA to the buyer
  • Lower operating costs: New owner inherits the electricity savings
  • No liability: We continue to own, operate, and maintain the system

Many commercial real estate buyers specifically look for properties with solar installations. It's a competitive advantage.
