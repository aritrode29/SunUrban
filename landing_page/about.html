We've all done this — most days we fight for the closest parking spot, but on a hot summer day, we'll go out of our way to park in the one spot with a sliver of tree shade.

A few summers ago here in Austin, I was leaving an H-E-B when I saw a middle-aged man collapse in the parking lot, strike his head on the concrete, and begin bleeding as his wife called out for help.

From what appeared to be heat exhaustion, the moment was alarming — and it stuck with me — because extreme heat isn't just uncomfortable, it's dangerous, and heat-related deaths are already the leading cause of climate-related deaths.

— Kendall Baker

Sun Urban was inspired by that reality.

While we solve real energy problems, human safety and livability were a primary motivation behind the idea.

Vision

To transform everyday urban surfaces into the backbone of a cooler, smarter, and more resilient energy system.

SunUrban envisions cities where parking lots, streets, and underused ground are no longer heat traps or dead space, but active infrastructure that:

  • generates clean power,
  • protects people from extreme heat,
  • supports electric mobility,
  • and strengthens the grid where demand actually exists.

The long-term vision is not solar adoption, but urban energy sovereignty where cities control their own ground-level energy layer.

Mission

To design, finance, and operate the urban canopy layer that converts parking infrastructure into clean energy, thermal comfort, and grid-ready assets.

SunUrban's mission is to:

  • intercept solar heat before it hits the ground,
  • turn shade into electricity,
  • deploy infrastructure with zero upfront cost to cities and institutions,
  • and progressively layer storage, EV charging, intelligence, and grid services over time.

SunUrban starts with parking lots because they are everywhere, politically feasible, and structurally ready, but the mission scales to any urban surface that can host energy, data, and comfort together.
